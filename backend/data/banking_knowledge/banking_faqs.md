# Banking FAQs & Policies

## KYC (Know Your Customer)

### What is KYC?
KYC is a mandatory process by RBI for verifying customer identity. All banks must complete KYC for every account holder.

### Documents Accepted
- **Officially Valid Documents (OVD)**: Aadhaar, Passport, Voter ID, Driving License, NREGA Job Card
- **Address Proof**: Utility bills (within 3 months), bank statement, registered lease agreement
- **PAN Card**: Mandatory for transactions above ₹50,000

### KYC Types
- **Full KYC**: In-person verification with original documents
- **e-KYC**: Aadhaar-based electronic verification
- **Video KYC (V-KYC)**: Remote verification via video call, approved by RBI
- **Central KYC (CKYC)**: Centralized registry - KYC done once, valid everywhere

### KYC Update
- Must be updated periodically (every 2 years for high-risk, 8 years for low-risk)
- Can be done at branch, online, or via video KYC
- Account may be frozen if KYC is not updated

## Fixed Deposit (FD)

### Overview
A fixed deposit is a financial instrument where money is deposited for a fixed tenure at a predetermined interest rate. Higher returns than savings accounts.

### Key Features
- Tenure: 7 days to 10 years
- Interest rates: 3% to 8% per annum (varies by bank and tenure)
- Senior citizens get 0.25% to 0.50% extra
- Premature withdrawal: Allowed with 0.5% to 1% penalty
- Loan against FD: Up to 90% of FD value at 1-2% above FD rate
- Tax-saving FD: 5-year lock-in, eligible for Section 80C deduction (up to ₹1.5 lakh)

### Tax on FD Interest
- TDS: 10% deducted if interest exceeds ₹40,000/year (₹50,000 for senior citizens)
- Submit Form 15G/15H to avoid TDS if total income is below taxable limit
- Interest is taxable as per income tax slab

## Recurring Deposit (RD)

### Overview
Monthly fixed installment deposit for a specified tenure. Good for disciplined saving.

### Features
- Monthly deposit: ₹100 to any amount
- Tenure: 6 months to 10 years
- Interest rates: Similar to FD rates
- Premature closure: Allowed with penalty
- Default in monthly deposit: Fine of ₹1 to ₹2 per ₹100 per month

## Current Account
- For businesses and frequent transactions
- No limit on transactions
- Minimum balance: ₹10,000 to ₹1,00,000
- No interest earned on balance
- Overdraft facility available
- Cheque book with more leaves

## Nomination
- Nominate a person to receive account balance in case of account holder's death
- Can be done during account opening or later
- Maximum one nominee per account
- Nominee can be changed anytime via form or online
- Nomination is not a substitute for a will

## Account Closure
- Visit branch with written request and passbook/cheque book
- Online closure available at some banks
- Outstanding dues must be cleared
- Linked services (auto-debit, standing instructions) must be cancelled
- Closure charges: ₹0 to ₹500 (if closed within 1 year of opening)

## Dormant/Inactive Account
- Account becomes inactive if no customer-initiated transaction for 12 months
- Becomes dormant after 24 months of inactivity
- To reactivate: Visit branch with KYC documents
- Interest continues to be credited
- Unclaimed deposits (10+ years): Transferred to RBI's DEAF fund

## Banking Ombudsman
- Free dispute resolution mechanism by RBI
- File complaint if bank doesn't resolve issue within 30 days
- Covers: Service charges, delays, unauthorized transactions, loans, credit cards
- Maximum compensation: ₹20 lakh
- Online filing at https://cms.rbi.org.in
- Time limit: Within 1 year of bank's final response

## Deposit Insurance (DICGC)
- All bank deposits insured up to ₹5 lakh per depositor per bank
- Covers: Savings, FD, RD, current accounts
- Premium paid by banks, not customers
- Includes principal and interest up to ₹5 lakh
- Available across all commercial and cooperative banks

## Locker Facility
- Safe deposit lockers for storing valuables
- Annual rent: ₹1,000 to ₹20,000 depending on size and location
- Sizes: Small, Medium, Large, Extra Large
- Nomination facility available
- Bank not liable for locker contents (but must maintain security)
- Breaking open: If rent unpaid for 3+ years, after due notice
- Insurance: Customer must insure own contents

## Grievance Redressal
1. **Level 1**: Contact bank's customer care (phone, email, branch)
2. **Level 2**: Escalate to bank's Grievance Redressal Officer
3. **Level 3**: File complaint with Banking Ombudsman (RBI)
4. **Level 4**: Approach consumer court

### Timeline
- Bank must acknowledge complaint within 3 working days
- Resolution within 30 days
- If unresolved, escalate to Ombudsman within 1 year

## Anti-Money Laundering (AML)
- Banks must report suspicious transactions to FIU (Financial Intelligence Unit)
- Cash transactions above ₹10 lakh must be reported
- Multiple cash transactions aggregating ₹10 lakh in a month are flagged
- PAN mandatory for cash deposits/withdrawals above ₹50,000 in a day

## Senior Citizen Banking Benefits
- Higher FD/savings interest rates (0.25% to 1% extra)
- Priority service at branches
- Doorstep banking facility
- Lower minimum balance requirements
- Higher TDS exemption limit (₹50,000 vs ₹40,000)
- Special loan schemes at concessional rates
