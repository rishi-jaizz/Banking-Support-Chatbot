# Credit Cards – Complete Information Guide

## What is a Credit Card?
A credit card is a financial instrument issued by banks that allows cardholders to borrow funds up to a pre-approved credit limit for making purchases, paying bills, or withdrawing cash. The borrowed amount must be repaid within the billing cycle or in monthly installments with interest.

## Types of Credit Cards

### Rewards Credit Card
- Earn reward points on every transaction (typically 1-5 points per ₹100 spent)
- Points can be redeemed for merchandise, vouchers, or cashback
- Annual fee: ₹0 to ₹2,000
- Best for: Regular spenders who want to maximize rewards on everyday purchases

### Cashback Credit Card
- Direct cashback of 1% to 5% on purchases
- Cashback may be category-specific (groceries, fuel, online shopping)
- Monthly/quarterly cashback caps apply (usually ₹500 to ₹2,000)
- Annual fee: ₹0 to ₹1,500
- Best for: People who prefer straightforward monetary returns

### Travel Credit Card
- Earn air miles or travel points on spending
- Complimentary airport lounge access (domestic and international)
- Travel insurance coverage up to ₹1 crore
- Foreign currency markup: 1.5% to 3.5%
- Annual fee: ₹1,000 to ₹10,000
- Best for: Frequent travelers

### Fuel Credit Card
- 1% to 5% fuel surcharge waiver at partner petrol pumps
- Additional reward points on fuel purchases
- Typically no fuel surcharge for transactions between ₹400 and ₹4,000
- Annual fee: ₹0 to ₹1,000
- Best for: People with high monthly fuel expenses

### Premium/Super Premium Credit Card
- High credit limit (₹5 lakh to ₹25 lakh+)
- Unlimited airport lounge access worldwide
- Complimentary golf sessions, spa access
- Dedicated relationship manager
- Travel and lifestyle benefits
- Annual fee: ₹5,000 to ₹50,000+
- Best for: High-income individuals seeking luxury benefits

### Secured Credit Card
- Issued against a fixed deposit (FD) as collateral
- Credit limit: 75% to 90% of FD amount
- Ideal for building or rebuilding credit score
- Interest rate: 15% to 30% per annum
- Annual fee: ₹0 to ₹500
- Best for: First-time credit card users or those with low credit score

### Student Credit Card
- Designed for college students
- Low credit limit: ₹15,000 to ₹1,00,000
- No income proof required (parent as guarantor)
- Reward points on education-related spending
- Annual fee: Usually ₹0
- Best for: Students learning financial management

## Credit Card Charges and Fees

### Interest Charges
- **APR (Annual Percentage Rate)**: 24% to 42% per annum (2% to 3.5% per month)
- Interest is charged on the outstanding balance if full payment is not made by the due date
- Interest-free period: 20 to 50 days from transaction date (only if previous balance is fully paid)
- Cash advance interest: Charged from the date of withdrawal with no interest-free period

### Common Fees
| Fee Type | Typical Amount |
|----------|---------------|
| Annual/Joining Fee | ₹0 to ₹50,000 |
| Late Payment Fee | ₹100 to ₹1,300 |
| Over-Limit Fee | 2.5% of over-limit amount |
| Cash Advance Fee | 2.5% to 3.5% of amount (min ₹250 to ₹500) |
| Foreign Currency Markup | 1.5% to 3.5% |
| Balance Transfer Fee | 1% to 3% |
| Card Replacement Fee | ₹100 to ₹500 |
| EMI Conversion Fee | 1% to 2% |
| GST | 18% on all fees and interest |

### Minimum Amount Due (MAD)
- Typically 5% of total outstanding or ₹200, whichever is higher
- Paying only MAD avoids late payment fee but incurs interest on entire outstanding
- Revolving credit: Interest is charged on all transactions from the date of transaction
- **Important**: Paying only minimum due is expensive and can lead to a debt trap

## Credit Card Billing Cycle

### Statement Generation
- Monthly statement generated on a fixed date (statement date)
- Contains all transactions from previous billing cycle
- Shows total outstanding, minimum due, and due date
- Due date is typically 15-20 days after statement date

### Payment Options
1. **Full payment**: Pay total outstanding by due date → no interest charged
2. **Minimum payment**: Pay at least MAD → no late fee but interest applies
3. **Partial payment**: Pay more than MAD → interest on remaining balance
4. **Auto-debit**: Set up standing instruction for automatic payment

## Credit Limit

### Factors Determining Credit Limit
- Monthly/annual income
- Existing debt obligations
- Credit score (CIBIL)
- Employment stability
- Relationship with the bank

### Credit Limit Enhancement
- Usually reviewed every 6-12 months
- Can request increase online or via phone
- Temporary limit enhancement available for special occasions
- Responsible usage and timely payments increase chances of limit enhancement
- Typical initial limits: ₹25,000 to ₹5,00,000

## Credit Score Impact

### Positive Factors
- Paying full outstanding before due date
- Keeping credit utilization below 30%
- Maintaining long credit history
- Having a mix of credit types

### Negative Factors
- Late payments or defaults
- High credit utilization (above 50%)
- Multiple credit card applications in short period
- Maxing out credit limits
- Settlement instead of full payment

## EMI on Credit Card
- Convert large purchases to EMIs (3 to 24 months)
- Interest rates: 12% to 24% per annum
- Processing fee: 0.5% to 2% of transaction amount
- No-cost EMI: Available on select merchants (interest borne by merchant)
- Pre-approved EMI offers available on many cards

## Security Features
- **CVV**: 3-digit security code on card back
- **OTP**: One-time password for online transactions (mandatory in India)
- **EMV Chip**: Secure chip-based transactions at POS
- **Contactless Payment**: Tap-and-go for transactions up to ₹5,000
- **Virtual Card Number**: For secure online transactions
- **Transaction Alerts**: SMS and email alerts for every transaction
- **Card Lock/Unlock**: Instant card blocking via mobile app
- **International Usage**: Must be enabled separately via app/net banking

## Dispute and Chargeback
- Report unauthorized transactions within 3 working days for zero liability
- File dispute through net banking, mobile app, or customer care
- Bank investigates and provides provisional credit within 10 working days
- Resolution timeline: 30 to 90 days
- RBI mandates that customer liability for unauthorized transactions:
  - Zero liability if reported within 3 days
  - Maximum ₹10,000 if reported within 4-7 days
  - Full liability if reported after 7 days
