# Savings Account – Complete Guide

## What is a Savings Account?
A savings account is a deposit account held at a bank or financial institution that provides a modest interest rate. It is designed for individuals who want to save money while earning interest on their deposits. Savings accounts are one of the most common and fundamental banking products available.

## Types of Savings Accounts

### Regular Savings Account
A basic savings account with standard features. Minimum balance requirements typically range from ₹500 to ₹10,000 depending on the bank. Interest rates range from 2.5% to 4% per annum. This account is suitable for individuals who want a simple, no-frills savings option.

### Zero Balance Savings Account
Also known as Basic Savings Bank Deposit Account (BSBDA). No minimum balance requirement. Limited number of free transactions per month, usually 4 withdrawals. Available under Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) for financial inclusion.

### Salary Account
Opened by employers for crediting employee salaries. Typically has zero balance requirement as long as salary is credited regularly. If salary stops being credited for 3 consecutive months, the account may be converted to a regular savings account. Comes with additional benefits like higher ATM withdrawal limits and free insurance coverage.

### Women's Savings Account
Specially designed for women with additional benefits like lower minimum balance, higher interest rates, and discounts on locker charges. Some banks offer complimentary insurance and shopping discounts.

### Senior Citizens Savings Account
For individuals aged 60 years and above. Offers 0.5% to 1% higher interest rate compared to regular savings accounts. Additional benefits include priority banking services and doorstep banking facilities.

### Minor's Savings Account
For children below 18 years of age. Can be operated by a guardian until the child turns 10, after which the minor can operate it independently. Teaches financial literacy and savings habits from a young age.

## Key Features of Savings Accounts

### Interest Rates
- Interest is calculated on daily closing balance
- Credited quarterly to the account
- Current rates range from 2.5% to 7% depending on the bank and balance
- Interest earned up to ₹10,000 per year is tax-free under Section 80TTA
- For senior citizens, the limit is ₹50,000 under Section 80TTB

### Minimum Balance Requirements
- Public sector banks: ₹500 to ₹3,000
- Private sector banks: ₹5,000 to ₹25,000
- Non-maintenance fee: ₹100 to ₹600 per quarter if balance falls below minimum

### Transaction Limits
- ATM withdrawals: 3-5 free transactions per month at own bank ATMs
- Other bank ATMs: 3-5 free transactions in metro cities, 5 in non-metro cities
- After free limit, charges of ₹20 per financial transaction and ₹8.50 per non-financial transaction

### Debit Card
- Complimentary debit card issued with the account
- Daily ATM withdrawal limit: ₹25,000 to ₹1,00,000
- Daily POS/online transaction limit: ₹50,000 to ₹2,00,000
- Cards come with RuPay, Visa, or Mastercard network

## How to Open a Savings Account

### Documents Required (KYC)
1. **Identity Proof**: Aadhaar Card, PAN Card, Voter ID, Passport, Driving License
2. **Address Proof**: Aadhaar Card, Utility Bills (not older than 3 months), Bank Statement, Passport
3. **Photographs**: 2 recent passport-sized photographs
4. **PAN Card**: Mandatory for accounts with annual transactions exceeding ₹50,000

### Opening Process
1. Visit the bank branch or apply online
2. Fill the account opening form
3. Submit KYC documents
4. Make initial deposit (if required)
5. Complete in-person verification (for online applications)
6. Receive account number, debit card, and internet banking credentials

### Video KYC
Many banks now offer Video KYC for opening accounts remotely. The process involves a video call with a bank representative who verifies your identity and documents digitally. This has been approved by RBI and is available 24/7 in most banks.

## Digital Banking Features
- Internet Banking: Check balance, transfer funds, pay bills, manage FD/RD
- Mobile Banking: Full banking on your smartphone with UPI integration
- UPI: Instant fund transfers using mobile number or UPI ID
- SMS Banking: Check balance and get mini-statements via SMS
- Email Alerts: Transaction notifications and monthly statements
